About Me

I am a PhD student nearing graduation at the College of Computer Science and Technology, Jilin University, under the supervision of Prof. Geng Sun. Prior to that, I received the B.S. degree from the College of Computer Science and Technology, Jilin University, in Jun. 2021. I have developed a NS3-based simulation platform for underwater acoustic sensor networks named "Aqua-Psim" [Project link], which provides accurate modeling and simulation of packet transmissions within underwater acoustic sensor networks, and supports users to design and verify their own protocols and algorithms. Research Direction

AI for Low-Altitude Secure Wireless Networks

This research is motivated by the need to bridge artificial intelligence (AI) with the unique security challenges of low‑altitude wireless networks (LAWNs), where high mobility, open channels, and resource‑constrained aerial nodes create an unprecedented attack surface. Conventional security mechanisms relying on static cryptography or rule‑based detection fail to adapt to dynamic topologies, intelligent jamming, and spoofing attacks. Unlike traditional methods that depend on handcrafted features, AI offers powerful learning and reasoning capabilities to perceive network dynamics and respond to threats in real time. By leveraging AI to enable adaptive, lightweight, and collaborative security solutions, this research aims to develop intelligent frameworks that autonomously reconfigure defense strategies. Such an AI‑driven, environment‑aware paradigm provides a practical pathway to resilient and trustworthy aerial‑ground integrated communications.

Previous Research Direction

Aqua-Psim: A Semi-Physical Simulation Platform Based on NS3 for Underwater Acoustic Network

Based on Aqua-sim-ng, we have developed a new semi-physical simulation platform called Aqua-Psim, which runs on NS-3.27. Figure shows that our platform consists of hardware and software. In terms of hardware, we use a DSP-based acoustic modulator as an external device, which can be replaced according to user requirements. The water tank provides a realistic underwater communication environment. The Linux host represents the communication intermediary between the actual modulator and the simulation nodes. In Aqua-Psim, the modulator obtains the messages sent by the simulation nodes through the socket program. Meanwhile, in the simulation part, a new module called AquaSim-TapBridge creates a virtual device TUN 0 to receive messages from the modulator. So far, the fourth generation has already been released.
